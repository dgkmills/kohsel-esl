Lesson 02: Short Vowel Sounds (Interactive)

Introduction to Short Vowels

Welcome to Lesson 2! Today, we will focus on **short vowel sounds**.

English has five vowels: A, E, I, O, U. Each vowel can make different sounds. Short vowel sounds are quick and clear, like a short tap.

Think of them like this:

  • A as in cat or apple
  • E as in bed or egg
  • I as in sit or igloo
  • O as in hot or octopus
  • U as in sun or umbrella

Minimal Pair Practice

"Minimal pairs" are words that sound almost the same, but one vowel sound is different. Listening carefully to these helps train your ear.

Pair Word 1 Word 2
A / E bad (adjective: not good) bed (noun: furniture for sleep)
I / E pin (noun: small sharp metal) pen (noun: tool for writing)
A / U ran (verb: past of run) run (verb: to move fast)
O / U cop (noun: police officer) cup (noun: container for drink)
I / A sit (verb: rest on a chair) sat (verb: past of sit)
I / A fin (noun: part of a fish) fan (noun: machine that moves air)

Practice saying these pairs aloud!
