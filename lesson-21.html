Lesson 21: Small Talk & Socializing in the Office (Interactive)

I. Review: Sequential Language

Let's review Lesson 20! Remember using words like First, Next, and Finally to explain a process.

Quick Challenge: Put these 3 steps for making coffee in the correct order.

  • A: Pour hot water
  • B: Add coffee
  • C: Get a cup

Correct Answer:

First, get a cup (C). Next, add coffee (B). Finally, pour hot water (A).

Clear steps are important, and today we'll learn how to start a clear *conversation*.

II. What is "Small Talk"?

"Small talk" is the polite, friendly conversation we have with colleagues. It is not about serious work problems.

Why is it important?

  • It helps build good, friendly relationships.
  • It makes the workplace more comfortable.
  • It shows respect to your colleagues.

We must use "safe" topics and avoid "unsafe" topics.

✅ "Safe" Topics

  • The Weather
  • The Weekend
  • Food / Canteen
  • Hobbies (movies, sports)
  • General Work (e.g., "busy week?")

❌ Topics to Avoid

  • Salary (Money)
  • Age ("How old are you?")
  • Politics / Religion
  • Personal Problems

III. Choral Reading: Key Phrases

Let's practice some key phrases. Please listen and repeat.

Starting a Conversation (Openers)

  • "How are you doing today?"
  • "Did you have a good weekend?"
  • "Do you have any plans for the weekend?"
  • "This weather is very (hot/nice), isn't it?"

Keeping the Conversation Going (Replies)

  • "I'm doing well, thank you. How about you?"
  • "It was good, thanks. I just relaxed at home."
  • "Not yet, maybe I'll just rest. And you?"
  • "Yes, it is! I hope it rains soon."
  • The most important reply: "Oh, really? That sounds (nice/interesting)."

IV. Game: Find Someone Who...

This is an activity to practice starting a conversation.

How to Play:

  • I will give you a printable card (or show this list on the screen).
  • Walk around the room.
  • Ask your colleagues "small talk" questions to find a person for each item on your card. Write their name.
  • Example: To find "watched a movie," you can ask, "Did you have a good weekend? Did you watch any movies?"

Prompts (on your card):

  • ...watched a movie this weekend.
  • ...ate something delicious today.
  • ...has weekend plans already.
  • ...is very busy this week.
  • ...likes spicy food.
  • ...thinks the weather is too hot.

The first person to find someone for 5 prompts wins!

V. Activity: Role-Play Practice

Now, let's practice in pairs.

How to Play:

  • Get with a partner.
  • Choose one scenario below.
  • Practice a 1-minute "small talk" conversation.
  • Remember to use our Starters and Replies!

Scenario 1: The Coffee Machine

You are waiting for your coffee. A colleague from another department is also waiting. Start a conversation.

Scenario 2: Waiting for a Meeting

You and your manager are in a room waiting for a meeting to start. It is quiet. Start a conversation.

Scenario 3: The Canteen

You are eating lunch. A colleague asks if they can sit at your table. Start a conversation.

VI. Wrap-up & Discussion

Great job today! You all did very well with the practice.

Key Takeaways:

  • Small talk is important for building good relationships.
  • Keep topics "safe": Weather, Weekends, Food, Hobbies.
  • Ask follow-up questions (like "Oh, really?") to show you are listening.
  • Practice makes you more confident!

Discussion:

Which "small talk" starter phrase do you feel most comfortable using? Why?
